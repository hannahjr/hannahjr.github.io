This application was developed as a part of the CS310 Computer Science Project at the University of Warwick. The aim of this project was to develop an application to enable teenagers to manage their diabetes easily, efficiently, and in the minimum amount of time. The application allows users to log their data, and then will present this data back in a visual and interactive format. One of the main focuses of the project will be to adapt these basic requirements to meet the needs of a user in their teens.
Awarded the Best Third Year Project 2017 on the BSc Computer Science degree at the University of Warwick.

User Testing

Task 1: User Interface Questionnaire
Users were provided with a range of screenshots of the application and asked a series of questions to establish whether the app's interface is intuitive and aesthetically pleasing.
Task 2: Functionality Questionnaire
Participants were asked a series of questions based around the functionality of the application to establish whether users believe the app will improve a user's diabetes management..
Task 3: Full Application Test and Interviews
Users were asked to use the application for a trial period and then provide any feedback (either positive or negative) for further development during the Final Refinements stage of the project.
